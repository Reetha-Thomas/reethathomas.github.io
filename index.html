About Me

I am currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Indian Institute of Management Bangalore (IIMB), specializing in climate modeling. I received my Ph.D. in Mathematics from the Indian Institute of Information Technology Kottayam in 2024 under the guidance of Dr. T. Bakkyaraj. My doctoral thesis, "Invariant Analysis and Exact Solutions of Certain Nonlinear Fractional Partial Differential and Differential-Difference Equations", focused on the analytical and numerical treatment of nonlinear systems governed by fractional dynamics.

My research expertise lies in nonlinear partial differential equations, with a strong emphasis on dynamical systems involving fractional and differential-difference equations. I have significantly contributed to the theoretical advancement of time-space fractional PDEs by extending analytical tools such as Lie Symmetry Analysis and the Invariant Subspace Method. Additionally, I have explored fuzzy differential equations and the mathematical modeling of SIRS-type epidemiological systems.

In recent years, I have expanded my focus to data-driven methodologies, particularly in integrating machine learning with mathematical modeling. I employ Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) and Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) to solve complex nonlinear differential equations, enabling accurate and interpretable predictions in applications like air quality modeling and spatio-temporal climate data analysis. This interdisciplinary approach bridges advanced mathematical theory with cutting-edge machine learning techniques.

Education

I hold a Ph.D. in Mathematics from the Indian Institute of Information Technology Kottayam (2020–2024), where I worked under the guidance of Dr. T. Bakkyaraj. My coursework included Fractional Differential Equations, Lie Symmetry Analysis, Invariant Subspace Method, and Differential and Differential-Difference Equations.

Prior to this, I completed an M.Phil. in Mathematics from Bharathiar University, Coimbatore (2017–2018), under the supervision of Dr. S. Narayanamoorthy. My M.Phil. thesis focused on the "Glucose-Insulin Regulatory Model Using Runge-Kutta and Homotopy Perturbation Methods in a Fuzzy Domain", and my coursework covered Fuzzy Mathematics, Partial Differential Equations, Algebra, and Analysis.

I also hold M.Sc. and B.Sc. degrees in Mathematics from Mahatma Gandhi University, Kottayam.
